Treating ourselves since 2020

How a fire swamp got into the bread business

"I like baking and hate racism."

It started in the early pandemic. The fancy in home dinner parties dried up overnight, daycare closed, and a friend was running a fundraiser called Bakers Against Racism. So I baked loaves and bagels, gave the proceeds to the NAACP Legal Defense Fund, and a little Facebook group called "Chef Alex's Weekly Treats" quietly turned into several hundred neighbors.

We never stopped. We still source the best ingredients we can find, mostly from the farms down the page, and we still give a percentage of everything back.

Before the bakery, that meant the California Culinary Academy, then years working up through Michelin starred kitchens (One Market and Acquerello), plus Moose's, Zuppa, Yoshi's, and opening Chow Danville as sous chef. The philosophy is simple. Get great ingredients, and don't mess them up.

"It's not that bad. The trees are actually quite lovely."

The name is from The Princess Bride, the fire swamp: a place full of well known terrors that somehow still has a little romance to it. Growing up, we watched it on repeat. Basically, I just love it.

Alex, Chef & Owner
